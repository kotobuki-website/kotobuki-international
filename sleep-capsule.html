The Birth of Capsule Hotel Culture

From Kisho Kurokawa's Vision to Global Standard

Capsule Inn Osaka, 1979

In 1979, Kotobuki partnered with legendary architect Kisho Kurokawa to create the world's first capsule hotel in Osaka. This revolutionary concept transformed how travelers and commuters experienced accommodation, proving that sophisticated comfort could be delivered in ultra-compact spaces.

Inspired by the efficiency of trains and capsule architecture, our team engineered the first proprietary capsule bed—combining Japanese precision manufacturing with innovative design that prioritized sleep quality, safety, and elegant simplicity.

What began as a single concept has grown into a global standard adopted by hotels, corporate offices, transit hubs, and wellness centers worldwide. We've installed over 60,000 units across 40+ years, continuously innovating to meet evolving market needs.
