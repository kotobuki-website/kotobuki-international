The Challenge

Project Overview

Guild Hall is one of America's most storied cultural institutions — a combined museum and theater that has hosted luminaries of the art world since 1931. A renovation of its intimate theater space called for seating that would honor the venue's historic character while providing the comfort and flexibility expected by today's audiences and performers.

The intimate scale of the theater meant every seat had to deliver an exceptional view and experience, with no margin for compromise on sight lines or finish quality.

Our Solution

What We Delivered

Working closely with Guild Hall's curatorial and architectural teams, Kotobuki designed seating that reflects the refined, understated aesthetic of the East Hampton institution. The upholstery palette and seat profiles were developed to complement the restored interiors while introducing a subtle modernity.

Particular attention was paid to ensuring that every seat in the house — from front row to rear — offered an unobstructed, intimate connection to the stage.

The Result

Outcome

Guild Hall's theater continues to be celebrated for its atmosphere — intimate, cultured, and beautifully appointed. The KSI installation has become an appreciated part of the audience experience, drawing comment from patrons and performers who note the quality and care evident in every detail.
